Automated Clinical Remediation Pipeline: Data Quality Engineering for Longitudinal Diabetes Records

Designed a modular Python-based Clinical Data Quality Pipeline to remediate systemic defects in the Diabetes 130‑US Hospitals dataset, transforming a decade of noisy longitudinal encounters into a high‑fidelity analytical asset. Engineered an automated Data Quality Index (DQI) framework, spanning completeness, validity, and consistency, that achieved a 25% improvement in overall data health while preserving all 101,766 patient encounters. Implemented Multivariate Imputation by Chained Equations (MICE) to resolve 97% missingness in critical attributes such as weight and payer code, preventing loss of statistical power and maintaining clinically plausible distributions. Standardized ICD‑9 diagnosis codes with Python’s Regular Expression library and validated remediation integrity via Kernel Density Estimate (KDE) plots, confirming that post‑processing distributions for variables like time in hospital remained statistically indistinguishable from the raw data. Delivered a scalable, SDLC‑driven framework and strategic roadmap that hospital stakeholders can reuse across service lines (e.g., Cardiology and Oncology) to support robust readmission‑risk modeling around the 11.2% early readmission cohort.

High-Volume Sentiment Engineering: IMDb Review Distillation for Automated Polarity Modeling

Engineered an end-to-end NLP pipeline to transform the 50,000‑record Stanford IMDb movie review corpus from 50,000 decentralized .txt files into a centralized, distillation‑grade Golden Asset for sentiment modeling. Automated high‑volume ingestion with directory‑level label engineering to maintain a perfectly balanced 25k/25k positive‑negative class split, then applied multi‑stage linguistic remediation to strip HTML artifacts, enforce normalization, and remove over 5.3 million low‑value tokens while preserving high‑intensity sentiment markers and negation. Implemented TF‑IDF bi‑gram vectorization with a 20,000‑feature cap and a distilled Logistic Regression model that achieved ≈89% accuracy and symmetric performance across positive and negative reviews. Validated semantic integrity through Semantic Word Clouds, Comparative Frequency Histograms, and confusion‑matrix visualizations, establishing a reusable SDLC‑driven framework for converting noisy, high‑volume text into a clinically styled, model‑ready asset for large‑scale sentiment auditing.
